 This example shows the effect of a longitudinal wave. The ideal crystal consists of a primitive cubic structure with one atom at 0,0,0. A longitudinal wave with wave vector displaces the atoms. The wave is characterized by: 

wave vector   [100]
wave length   27A     5.2 unit cells
amplitude     0.5A    0.1 unit cells

The left image shows a part of the crystal, 30*30 unit cells were used for the calculation. The right image shows one quadrant of reciprocal space.

The satellite vector is 0.19 0 0, parallel to the wave vector in real space. Its absolute value is inversely proportional to the wave length. The intensity of the satellite reflections depends on the amplitude of the displacement. The satellites around the 0k0 reflections are systematically extinct. Here the term h ( u - < u > ) equals zero and no deviation from the averaged structure can be observed. Imagine the structure projected onto the 010 line. This projection is identical to that of a undisturbed crystal and accordingly the 0k0 reflections are identical.
